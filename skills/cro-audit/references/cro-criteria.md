# CRO Evaluation Criteria by Page Type

This document contains the detailed evaluation criteria for each ecommerce page type.
Use these as your checklist when analyzing each page. Not every criterion applies to every site —
use judgment based on the store's vertical, price point, and target audience.

## Homepage

The homepage is often the first impression and needs to answer three questions within 5 seconds:
"What do you sell?", "Why should I buy from you?", and "What should I do next?"

### Criteria

**H1. Hero Section & Value Proposition**
- Is there a clear, benefit-driven headline (not just the brand name)?
- Does the hero image/video show the product in context?
- Is there a primary CTA that tells the user what to do next?
- Is the value proposition unique and specific (not generic like "best quality")?

**H2. Navigation & Information Architecture**
- Can users find major categories within 1-2 clicks?
- Is the navigation organized by user mental models (not internal taxonomy)?
- Is the mega menu (if present) well-organized with visual cues?
- Is there a visible search bar in the header?

**H3. Trust & Credibility (Above the Fold)**
- Are trust signals visible without scrolling? (reviews count, awards, press mentions, guarantees)
- Is there a clear indicator of shipping/return policies early on?
- Are any certifications, partnerships, or social proof visible?

**H4. Product Discovery**
- Does the homepage surface products or categories clearly?
- Are featured products shown with prices (not hidden behind a click)?
- Is there a "best sellers" or "trending" section to help undecided visitors?
- Are promotional banners clear and not overwhelming the layout?

**H5. Mobile Homepage Experience**
- Does the hero resize properly without losing the CTA?
- Is the mobile navigation (hamburger menu) easy to access and use?
- Are tap targets large enough (minimum 44x44px)?
- Does content load quickly and not shift around (layout stability)?

## Collection / Category Page

The collection page is where purchase intent meets product exploration. Its job is to help users
efficiently browse, filter, and find the right product.

### Criteria

**C1. Filtering & Sorting**
- Are relevant filters available (size, color, price, rating, etc.)?
- Can users apply multiple filters simultaneously?
- Is there a sort-by option (price, popularity, newest, rating)?
- Do filters show result counts to prevent dead ends?
- Do filters work without full page reload?

**C2. Product Cards**
- Do product cards show: image, title, price, rating/reviews count?
- Is the image large enough to see product detail?
- Is there a hover state or quick-view option?
- Are sale/discount prices clearly shown with original price crossed out?
- Is there a visual indicator for out-of-stock items?

**C3. Layout & Grid**
- Is the grid density appropriate? (not too cramped, not too sparse)
- Is pagination, infinite scroll, or "load more" implemented well?
- Is the product count visible ("Showing 24 of 156 products")?
- Does the layout adapt well on mobile (1-2 column grid)?

**C4. Category Navigation**
- Are subcategories visible and easy to access?
- Is there a breadcrumb trail for orientation?
- Can users easily switch between categories without going back to homepage?

**C5. Urgency & Social Proof on Cards**
- Are badges like "Best Seller", "New", "Low Stock" used effectively?
- Is there any social proof at the card level (review stars, "X sold")?
- Are any promotional tags visible (free shipping, sale)?

## Product Detail Page (PDP)

The PDP is the most critical page for conversion. Nearly every purchase decision passes through here.
Per Baymard research, 51% of ecommerce sites have mediocre or worse PDP UX.

### Criteria

**P1. Product Images & Media**
- Are there multiple high-quality images (minimum 3-4)?
- Can users zoom in to see product detail?
- Is there an image showing scale/context (product in use, on a person)?
- Is there a video or 360° view option?
- Do images load quickly and not cause layout shifts?

**P2. Product Title & Description**
- Is the title descriptive and scannable?
- Does the description lead with benefits, not just features?
- Is there a scannable format (bullet points, icons) for key specs?
- Is sizing/dimension information easy to find?
- Is there a size guide (for apparel/accessories)?

**P3. Price & Availability**
- Is the price prominently displayed near the Add to Cart button?
- If on sale, is the original price, sale price, and savings shown?
- Is availability/stock status clearly indicated?
- Are any quantity discounts or bundles shown?
- Is shipping cost/estimate visible before checkout?

**P4. Add to Cart (Primary CTA)**
- Is the Add to Cart button the most visually prominent element on the page?
- Is it above the fold on desktop?
- Does it use a high-contrast color that stands out from the page?
- Is the button text clear ("Add to Cart" vs. ambiguous text)?
- Does it provide clear feedback when clicked (animation, cart count update, mini-cart)?

**P5. Variant Selection**
- Are product variants (size, color, material) easy to select?
- Do variant selectors show availability per variant?
- Do images update when a variant is selected?
- Is the selected variant clearly indicated?

**P6. Trust Signals & Risk Reducers**
- Are trust badges visible near the CTA? (secure checkout, money-back guarantee)
- Is the return/exchange policy clearly stated on the PDP?
- Is shipping information (cost, speed) visible?
- Are payment method icons shown?

**P7. Social Proof & Reviews**
- Are customer reviews visible on the PDP?
- Is the average rating shown near the top of the page?
- Can users filter reviews by star rating, verified purchase, etc.?
- Are there user-submitted photos in reviews?
- Is the review count sufficient to build confidence?

**P8. Cross-Sells & Upsells**
- Are related products or "frequently bought together" shown?
- Are recommendations relevant to the current product?
- Are they positioned to enhance (not distract from) the purchase decision?

**P9. Mobile PDP Experience**
- Is the Add to Cart button sticky or easily accessible while scrolling?
- Are images swipeable and large enough on mobile?
- Can users easily expand/collapse product details?
- Is the page length manageable on mobile (not excessively long)?

## Cart Page

The cart page is a critical conversion point — it's where users decide whether to proceed to checkout
or abandon. The primary job is to summarize the order clearly and provide a frictionless path to checkout.

### Criteria

**CT1. Order Summary Clarity**
- Is each item shown with: image, name, variant, price, quantity?
- Can users easily modify quantity or remove items?
- Is the subtotal clearly displayed?
- Are shipping costs estimated (or is "free shipping" indicated)?
- Are any taxes or fees shown (or noted as calculated at checkout)?

**CT2. Cart CTA & Checkout Path**
- Is the "Proceed to Checkout" button prominent and high-contrast?
- Is there both a top and bottom checkout CTA (for longer carts)?
- Are express checkout options available (Apple Pay, Google Pay, PayPal, Shop Pay)?
- Is there a "Continue Shopping" link that doesn't feel like the primary action?

**CT3. Trust & Anxiety Reduction**
- Are return policy reminders present?
- Are security/payment trust badges visible?
- Is there a money-back guarantee or similar risk reducer?
- Are shipping timelines indicated?

**CT4. Cart Upsells & Cross-Sells**
- Are there relevant upsell or cross-sell suggestions?
- Are they helpful (not annoying or distracting)?
- Is there a free shipping threshold indicator ("Add $15 more for free shipping")?

**CT5. Promo Code Handling**
- Is the promo code field visible but not overly prominent?
- Does applying a code provide immediate feedback on savings?
- An overly prominent coupon field can cause users to leave and search for codes — is this managed well?

**CT6. Cart Persistence & Recovery**
- Does the cart persist across sessions (for logged-in and guest users)?
- Is there a save-for-later option?

## Checkout Page

Checkout is the final conversion gate. Baymard research shows the average large ecommerce site can gain
a 35% increase in conversion just by improving checkout UX. The goal: minimize friction, reduce anxiety,
and make completing the purchase feel effortless.

### Criteria

**CK1. Form Design & Fields**
- Are only necessary fields present (no excessive data collection)?
- Are fields properly labeled with inline validation?
- Is there auto-detection (e.g., city from zip code)?
- Are there sensible defaults?
- Is the keyboard type appropriate on mobile (numeric for phone/zip)?

**CK2. Guest Checkout**
- Is guest checkout available and prominent?
- Is account creation optional (not forced before purchase)?
- If account creation is offered, is it framed as a benefit (e.g., "save for faster checkout next time")?

**CK3. Progress Indication**
- Is there a progress bar or step indicator?
- Can users see how many steps remain?
- Can users go back to previous steps without losing data?

**CK4. Payment Options**
- Are multiple payment methods available?
- Are express payment options (Apple Pay, Google Pay, etc.) offered early?
- Is "buy now pay later" available for higher-priced items?
- Are payment method logos visible?

**CK5. Order Summary Visibility**
- Is the order summary visible throughout checkout?
- Can users see what they're buying without leaving the checkout flow?
- Are the total, shipping, and taxes clearly broken down?

**CK6. Security & Trust**
- Does the checkout page look secure (HTTPS, security badges)?
- Is the page free of distracting elements (no full site nav, no promo banners)?
- Is there a clear privacy statement or link near sensitive fields?

**CK7. Error Handling**
- Are form errors shown inline next to the relevant field?
- Are error messages helpful and specific (not generic "invalid input")?
- Does the form preserve entered data if an error occurs?

**CK8. Mobile Checkout**
- Is the checkout fully optimized for mobile (no horizontal scrolling)?
- Are buttons large enough for touch?
- Is autofill properly supported?
- Can the user complete checkout without zooming?

## Search & Search Results

Site search users typically convert at 2-3x the rate of browsers, so a good search experience
is critical for ecommerce conversion.

### Criteria

**S1. Search Accessibility**
- Is the search bar visible and prominent in the header?
- Does it have a placeholder hint ("Search products, brands...")?
- Is it easily accessible on mobile?

**S2. Search Intelligence**
- Does search support autocomplete/suggestions?
- Does it handle typos and synonyms?
- Are popular or trending searches suggested?

**S3. Results Quality**
- Are results relevant to the query?
- Are results displayed with: image, title, price, rating?
- Can results be filtered and sorted?
- Is there a "no results" experience with helpful alternatives?

## Cross-Cutting Technical Criteria

Evaluate these across all pages:

**T1. Mobile Responsiveness**
- Do all pages render correctly at 390px width?
- Are tap targets adequately sized (44x44px minimum)?
- Is text readable without zooming?
- Are images properly sized for mobile (not desktop images scaled down)?

**T2. Page Load Performance**
- Did any page feel noticeably slow during browsing?
- Are there visible layout shifts as the page loads?
- Are images lazy-loaded below the fold?
- Are large hero images optimized?

**T3. Accessibility Basics**
- Do primary CTA buttons have sufficient color contrast?
- Do images have alt text?
- Are form inputs properly labeled?
- Can the site be navigated with a keyboard?

**T4. Cross-Browser/Device Consistency**
- Note any rendering issues observed during the audit
- Flag any JavaScript errors visible in browser console
